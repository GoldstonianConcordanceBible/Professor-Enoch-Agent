Decentralized Theology

Decentralized Theology describes a shift in theological study away from only institution-controlled interpretation and toward open, distributed knowledge systems.

This model includes:

open datasets  
public commentary  
AI-assisted scripture study  
decentralized research communities  
machine-readable theological systems

Within the GCB ecosystem, Decentralized Theology connects biblical study with modern infrastructure such as repositories, knowledge graphs, media, and AI agents.

The core idea is that theology in the digital age can be studied, explained, and distributed through open systems while remaining grounded in scripture.
